Schools for English-speaking families

For most families this is the question that decides the city. Jeddah has the deepest school market in the Hijaz, Madinah has solid Islamic international schools at gentler prices, and Makkah families often commute to Jeddah. Here are the schools we point people to first.

Makkah

The international-school market inside Makkah is small. Yusr International School (IB, bilingual English-Arabic, Al Hamra district) and Al Fursan International (American curriculum) are the main names. Many families in Makkah choose a school in Jeddah instead; the drive is 40 to 60 minutes and school buses cover it. Factor that commute into where you rent.

Things parents learn the hard way

Enrolment needs your children's iqamas, so the family visa comes first; see the visas guide. Attested previous-school records and vaccination cards speed everything up, so bring originals. Fees vary enormously, from a few thousand riyals a year at local international schools to figures matching British private schools at the big names, and the good schools in Jeddah hold waiting lists for popular year groups. Apply the term before you fly, not the week after you land. Homeschooling exists in the community here too; ask about it in the groups.
