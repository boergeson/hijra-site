Every legal way to live in Saudi Arabia, with prices

This is the page we wish existed when we started helping people with hijrah. Every route below is legal and real. Costs are in Saudi riyals (SAR 3.75 = 1 US dollar) and were checked in July 2026. Rules change, so confirm on the official links before you act.

Tourist visa: for scouting, not for staying

If you hold a passport from the UK, US, EU or about 60 other countries, or a used US, UK or Schengen visa, you can get a one-year multiple-entry eVisa in minutes for around SAR 535 including insurance. Each stay is capped at 90 days. You can make Umrah on it outside Hajj season.

What it does not give you: an iqama, the right to work, a local bank account, or a proper rental contract. Use it the smart way: come for a month, see Jeddah and Madinah with your own eyes, look at schools and apartments, pray in the Haram, then plan properly. Apply only at the official portal, visa.visitsaudi.com. Copycat sites charge markups for nothing.

Do not overstay. Some brothers arrive on a tourist or Umrah visa and plan to "sort it out from inside". There is no way to convert a visit visa into residency in-country, and the Interior Ministry warns of fines up to SAR 50,000, jail and deportation for overstays. An amnesty might never come. Do it properly from the start.

Work visa and iqama: the classic route

You get a job offer, the employer arranges the work visa through the government's Qiwa system, you do the medical and document attestation in your country, and after arrival the employer converts your entry visa into a work permit and iqama, your residence card. See the jobs guide for where to find offers and which fields are open.

Know your rights before you sign. Under Article 40 of the Saudi Labor Law the employer pays for your visa, iqama issuance and renewals, and the expat levy. An employer who asks you to cover those is breaking the law. What you do pay is the dependent fee: SAR 400 per month for each family member you sponsor. For a wife and three children that is SAR 19,200 per year, and you should budget for it honestly.

To bring your family to live with you, you apply for the permanent family visa, SAR 2,000 one time for the whole family. One catch that surprises people: only skilled job titles can sponsor family. Check that your contract's job title qualifies before you sign, not after. We can check it for you.

Student residency: the door most people miss

A full scholarship at a Saudi university covers tuition, your iqama, housing, healthcare, an annual flight home and a monthly stipend. If you are between 17 and 25 this is the single most accessible route, and it comes with sacred knowledge. It deserves its own page: the student route.

Premium Residency: the "Saudi green card"

Premium Residency means you live in Saudi Arabia without a sponsor. You can work for any private employer, run businesses, own property in permitted areas, sponsor your wife, children and parents, and come and go freely. No dependent fees either. Since 2024 there are seven versions:

Read that table again. A Muslim doctor or senior nurse earning SAR 35,000 a month can get five-year sponsor-free residency for a SAR 4,000 fee. So can someone who buys a SAR 4 million apartment, which since 2026 can be in approved zones of Makkah or Madinah itself; see the housing guide. The official portal is pr.gov.sa. Approval usually takes 30 to 90 days.

Family visit visa

Once you hold an iqama you can invite your parents or in-laws for visits through visa.mofa.gov.sa: single entry up to 90 days and extendable monthly, or multiple entry for a year with 90 days per stay. A visit visa is not residency, and the sponsor is liable if a guest overstays.

A word about citizenship

Be clear-eyed about this: Saudi Arabia is not a naturalisation country. Citizenship is granted rarely, by royal decree, mostly to selected scientists, doctors and scholars, and dual nationality is not permitted for adults who naturalise. For a Muslim family, "settling permanently" means an indefinitely renewed iqama or Premium Residency, not a passport. That is the reality, and alhamdulillah it is enough: your children grow up praying in the Haram either way.

The shortcuts that end in deportation

The "free visa" (azad visa). A Saudi sells you a work visa, you work wherever you want and pay him yearly. There is no such visa category. It is visa trading, it is illegal, and the penalties reach SAR 100,000, jail, deportation and a re-entry ban. You also have zero protection when your "sponsor" decides to blackmail you, and it happens.

Business fronting (tasattur). Running a business registered in a Saudi citizen's name is a crime under the Anti-Concealment Law: up to five years in prison, fines up to SAR 5 million, confiscation, and for the foreigner deportation with a permanent ban. There is a legal way to own your business 100%; it is on the business page.

People in the groups will tell you "everyone does it". Everyone also gets caught eventually, and enforcement has been serious since 2021. Your hijrah should not start with a contract Allah is not pleased with and end with a deportation stamp.
