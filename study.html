The student route: a full scholarship and legal residency

If you are between 17 and 25, this is the best door into Saudi Arabia, full stop. The government pays for everything, the university sponsors your iqama, and you spend your years studying, often at institutions a short walk from the Haram. Here is how it actually works in 2026.

What the scholarship includes

The standard full government scholarship covers tuition, your iqama for the length of your studies, furnished housing, healthcare, a flight ticket when you are admitted plus a round trip home every year, and a monthly stipend of roughly SAR 850–900 for bachelor's students (about SAR 1,900 for postgraduates). At the Islamic University of Madinah there is also subsidised food and free daily buses to the Prophet's Mosque ﷺ.

The stipend is pocket money, not a salary. You will live simply, like students everywhere. But you will owe nothing, and your legal status is clean for the entire degree.

One portal for everything

Since the Ministry of Education centralised admissions, all international applications run through the Study in Saudi Arabia platform (studyinsaudi.moe.gov.sa). One free account, one application, and you rank your preferred programs across about 27 public universities. The universities themselves, including Madinah and Imam University, now direct foreign applicants there instead of their own old portals.

The 2026 bachelor's window ran from 19 April to 21 May, with acceptances through June and visas over the summer for a September start. If you are reading this after the window closed, use the year: gather your documents, start Arabic, and apply the day it opens. We can watch the dates for you.

Documents you will need

A valid passport, your attested high school certificate and transcript, a birth certificate, a recent criminal record check, a medical report and a photo. Madinah has historically also asked for a tazkiyah, a recommendation from an Islamic centre or known person of knowledge. Getting attestations done takes weeks, so start early.

The rules that decide everything

Age limits are hard limits. Bachelor's and the Arabic institute: 17 to 25. Master's: under 30. PhD: under 35. If you are 26, the bachelor's scholarship door is closed, and anyone who tells you otherwise is guessing. Degree holders should look at the postgraduate tracks instead, and there are also self-funded places without the full package. Ask us about your case.

No Arabic? Not a problem, and this surprises people. Every major university runs an Arabic institute for non-native speakers, commonly a two-year program that takes you from zero to degree-ready, and scholarship students at the institute get the full package from day one. Thousands of Western students started exactly this way.

For the sisters: the Islamic University of Madinah's undergraduate scholarships are for men. Sisters apply to Umm Al-Qura in Makkah, Imam University in Riyadh, King Abdulaziz in Jeddah, Taibah in Madinah, or Princess Nourah in Riyadh, the largest women's university in the world.

Married? Read this first

Standard scholarship housing is for single students, and whether a student can sponsor his wife and children is the least settled question in this whole system. The new educational visa is described as allowing dependents; in practice implementation varies by university and by year. Married brothers have brought families to Madinah for decades, but do not build your plan on it until it is confirmed for your case. This is exactly the kind of thing we check with people on the ground before you commit. Ask us.

Already here on another visa?

If you have residency through work or business, there are internal scholarship tracks at some universities, evening Islamic institutes, and the study circles of the Two Haramain which are free and open to everyone. The famous institutes of the Grand Mosque in Makkah and the Prophet's Mosque in Madinah are real and give recognised certificates, but their recent intakes required existing Saudi residency. Studying is something you do after your legal status is sorted, not a way to sort it.
