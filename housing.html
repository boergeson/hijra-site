Housing: renting today, and the new right to buy

Most families rent first, and renting in the Hijaz is cheaper than people expect. Buying used to be closed to foreigners almost everywhere. That changed in January 2026, and for Muslims it changed most of all.

What rent costs

CityTwo-bedroom apartment, monthlyNotes
JeddahSAR 3,200–5,500Average around 3,950; rising a few percent a year
MakkahSAR 1,500–6,000Spikes near the Haram and in Hajj season
MadinahSAR 1,000–4,000The most affordable of the three

Rent is traditionally paid in one or two large payments a year, not monthly, so budget for that on arrival. Monthly payment plans are spreading but are not yet the norm.

Search on Aqar (the biggest local portal), Bayut (best English interface), Wasalt and Property Finder. Listings in the community groups are how many brothers actually find places, especially in Madinah.

The Ejar contract protects you, use it

Every residential lease must be registered on Ejar, the government rental platform. A registered contract means your rent cannot be raised mid-term, eviction needs a legal process, and your deposit has rules. A landlord who wants to skip Ejar is a landlord to avoid. Note that in practice you need an iqama to register one, which is another reason a visit visa is not a way to live here.

Buying: the 2026 change

A new property law took effect in January 2026, and the zone maps were approved in June 2026. Non-Saudis, resident or abroad, can now buy in designated zones, initially centred on Riyadh and Jeddah. A foreigner living here on an iqama can also own one home outside the zones. The transaction fee on foreign purchases is up to 5%, and everything runs through the official REGA platform, rega.gov.sa, which publishes the interactive zone maps.

Makkah and Madinah are the headline. Ownership in the holy cities is open to Muslims only, in approved zones, whether you live in Saudi Arabia or abroad. For decades this was simply impossible. A Muslim in Birmingham or Chicago can now, in principle, own an apartment in Makkah. The approved zones are mostly the big new developments around the Haramain, and prices there are serious, but the door exists and it is halal ownership with registered title.

Property that comes with residency

Own developed residential property worth SAR 4 million or more, fully paid and mortgage-free, and you qualify for the real-estate track of Premium Residency for a SAR 4,000 fee. The residency lasts as long as you own qualifying property, and it covers your family. For a family with savings from selling a house in the West, this is a route worth pricing out properly: it combines the home and the legal status in one purchase.

Buy with your eyes open. Off-plan does not count for the residency track, valuations must come from an accredited appraiser, and zone boundaries decide everything. Do not send money to anyone for "pre-launch Makkah apartments" without verifying the project against the REGA map. We check listings and developers for clients before a single riyal moves.
